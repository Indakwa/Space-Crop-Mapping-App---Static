    • Njoro Wheat Crop Mapping Using Artificial Intelligence and Satellite Imaging Technology

    Mapping of crops is important to various stakeholders; Researchers need such information to estimate the impact of climate change on agriculture, calculate yield gaps (difference between actual and potential yields), or analyze the historical evolution of farming systems. Policymakers, donors, and investors need it to better target agricultural and rural development investments and policies. In this project we propose to map a wheat farm by using the combination of optical vegetation indices and synthetic aperture radar (SAR) data. The Sentinel satellites imagery will used to identify Wheat farm with a high temporal and spatial resolution. Vegetation indices such as normalized difference vegetation index (NDVI) and enhanced vegetation index (EVI) will be estimated from optical bands. (VH (vertical-horizontal) and VV (vertical-vertical)) Polarization bands will be used to overcome the cloud contamination problem. In conjunction with the random forest machine learning algorithm on the Google Earth Engine platform, mapping of a wheat farm in Njoro will be done as an experimental area
